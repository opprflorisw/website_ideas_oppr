Factories run on their operators. Not just their machines.

Two decades of digitization gave every plant a data layer for machines — and nothing for the people who actually run them. That knowledge lives in heads and retires. Oppr exists to capture it, and to build the intelligence layer on top.

Why now

The expert workforce is aging out, and every retirement takes thirty years of intuition with it. Input costs, energy and CO₂ are squeezing margins, so the firefighting has to stop. And for the first time, AI can actually capture and use what operators know.

The plants that capture both halves will run stable and compound knowledge every shift. The ones that don’t will keep deciding on gut feeling — with one eye closed.

Where this goes

We sell the loop that works today. But the timeline is the foundation for an operations intelligence layer — the Oppr OS — that climbs a ladder from capture to autonomy.
